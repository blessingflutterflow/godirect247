Key Terms and Conditions: Breakdown Waiting Periods: Generally, a 6-month waiting period applies for natural death, meaning no claims can be made within the first 6 months of the policy. Accidental death is often covered immediately. Waiting Periods for Specific Conditions: A 12-month waiting period usually applies for death due to suicide. Premiums and Lapsing: Policies require monthly premiums to be paid on time. A grace period, often 31 days, is provided to settle missed payments before the policy lapses (cancels). Beneficiaries: You can name or change beneficiaries at any time. If no beneficiary is alive at the time of the claim, the money is paid to the estate. Covered Lives: Policies may cover the main member, one spouse, and a limited number of children. Extended family members, such as parents or siblings, can often be added for an additional premium. Age Limits: Children are generally covered up to age 21, or up to 25 if they are full-time students. Parents and extended family often have entry age restrictions, sometimes up to 84 years old. Documentation: A valid claim requires documents like a death certificate, BI-1663 (or DHA-1663), and police reports for unnatural deaths, usually required within a certain timeframe (e.g., 6 months). Claim Payouts: Payouts are often made within 24 to 48 hours, depending on the insurer, after the required documentation is submitted. Limitations on Children: The payout amount for children is legally restricted based on their age (e.g., lower payouts for children under 6 or under 14 years old). *
